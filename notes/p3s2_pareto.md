## Honest interpretation (load-bearing for the paper story)

**1. The benchmark-conditional pattern is CONFIRMED but the discriminator is
NOT "answer density" — it is whether the proxy selector's r50 pruning costs
accuracy on that benchmark.**
- MME (yes/no, short): r50 acc 0.685 < r25 acc 0.690 -> r50 has a small acc
  cost -> adaptive (0.700) wins on acc AND req/s. PARETO-DOMINATES.
- ScienceQA (MC, hint+image): r50 acc 0.670 < r25 acc 0.675 -> r50 has an acc
  cost -> adaptive (0.685) wins on both. PARETO-DOMINATES.
- MMBench (MC): r50 acc 0.730 is the HIGHEST (>= r25 0.725) -> proxy pruning
  is acc-NEUTRAL/POSITIVE here -> fixed-r50 dominates on both axes. Adaptive
  loses. This is the SAME "free recovery" regime as GQA, not a method failure.

So the thesis "denser/longer-answer benchmarks favor adaptive" is too coarse:
MMBench is dense MC yet r50 is free there. The real predictor is the per-
benchmark r50-acc-cost. Adaptive's value proposition: it captures the r50
throughput gain where r50 is costly, and falls back to r25 where r50 is free
-> it is the safe choice when the deployer does not know a priori whether r50
costs accuracy on the target workload. (2/3 new + TextVQA n=200 = the costly
regime; GQA + MMBench = the free regime.)

**2. TextVQA n=500 REVERSED the P3-step-1 acc headline (IMPORTANT CORRECTION).**
- P3-step-1 n=200: adaptive 0.540 > r50 0.520 (+0.020) -> "clean Pareto-dominate".
- P3-step-2 n=500: adaptive 0.510 < r50 0.526 (-0.016, z=-0.51 not sig).
The n=200 +0.020 acc margin was NOISE; at n=500 the sign flips (also not
significant, but no longer in adaptive's favor). The P3-step-1 TextVQA
"clean win on both axes" claim is DOWNGRADED to "adaptive wins req/s over r25
only" — same status as GQA at n=200. The TextVQA req/s advantage (adaptive
2.270 > r25 2.233, +0.037) is the only robust TextVQA signal.

**Net for the paper:** the load-adaptive method's robust, benchmark-conditional
benefit is a THROUGHPUT win over the accuracy-favoring fixed point (r25) on
ALL 5 benchmarks (MME +0.045, MMBench +0.072, ScienceQA +0.070, GQA +0.024
[P3s1], TextVQA +0.037 [n=500]). The accuracy win over r50 is real only where
r50 is costly (MME, ScienceQA) and was noise on TextVQA. Frame the method as
"throughput-optimal under a per-benchmark accuracy guardrail, automatically
adapting the prune depth to load" rather than "Pareto-dominant".

**3. FastV anchor context.** FastV (in-LLM layer-2 attention-rank prune, keep
288) is accuracy-comparable to our projector-path proxy r50 on MME (0.720 vs
our 0.685) and ScienceQA (0.700 vs our 0.670), and higher on MMBench (0.740
vs our 0.730). FastV is the stronger accuracy baseline (it prunes inside the
LLM where task-relevance is clearer), but it CANNOT run in vLLM (FlashAttn
fuses softmax) -> no serving throughput. Our method trades a small accuracy
delta for vLLM compatibility + the load-adaptive throughput gain. This is the
positioning for related-work: complementary to FastV, not replacing it.

**4. Concurrency matrix (c1/c4/c12 served-throughput scaling).** r0: 1.815 ->
3.436 -> 5.754 req/s (c1->c4->c12 = 3.2x from c1 to c12). r50: 2.132 -> 5.022
-> 8.181 req/s (3.8x c1->c12). Compression's throughput benefit GROWS with
concurrency (r50/r0 = 1.17x at c1, 1.42x at c12) -> confirms the M2 finding
that KV-pressure relief amplifies with load. This is the headline efficiency
figure and the motivation for load-adaptive pruning.
